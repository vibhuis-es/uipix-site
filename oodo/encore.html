    Hotel Financial Management Platform

    Duration
    8 months
    Platforms
    Web & Mobile
    Role
    UX Designer

    Before

    → ​Spreadsheet-based budgeting

    → ​No centralized visibility

    → ​Difficult data retrieval

    → ​Manual report generation

    The Problem

    Hotel managers were drowning in spreadsheets. Financial planning happened across disconnected Excel files, making it nearly impossible to maintain centralized visibility into budgets, expenses, and forecasts. When executives needed historical data for decision-making, teams spent hours manually compiling reports.

    Without real-time analytics or a unified system, financial oversight was reactive rather than strategic. Budget allocation decisions were delayed, expense approvals got lost in email threads, and coordination with vendors suffered from fragmented communication.

    After

    ✓ ​Centralized platform

    ✓ ​Real-time data visibility

    ✓ ​Instant historical access

    ✓ ​Automated analytics

    Users & Context

    The platform needed to serve multiple roles across the hotel management hierarchy, each with distinct responsibilities and decision-making needs. Understanding these varied workflows was critical to designing an experience that felt intuitive for everyone.

    General Managers

    Primary Goals

    • Monitor property-level budgets
    • Approve expenses quickly
    • Track monthly performance

    Key Pain Points

    • No visibility into real-time spending
    • Approval requests lost in email
    • Manual data compilation for reports

    Regional Managers

    Primary Goals

    • Oversee multiple properties
    • Compare performance across hotels
    • Identify budget variances early

    Key Pain Points

    • Inconsistent data formats
    • Difficult to spot trends
    • Time-consuming cross-property analysis

    Administrative Teams

    Primary Goals

    • Enter budget data accurately
    • Submit expense requests
    • Coordinate vendor payments

    Key Pain Points

    • Error-prone spreadsheet entry
    • Unclear approval workflows
    • Fragmented vendor communication

    Vice Presidents

    Primary Goals

    • Strategic financial planning
    • Portfolio-wide visibility
    • Make data-driven decisions

    Key Pain Points

    • Delayed access to key metrics
    • No predictive analytics
    • Reliance on manually compiled reports

    Defining the Experience

    Through stakeholder interviews and contextual inquiry, we identified that successful financial management in hospitality required role-based access, scenario-driven design, and workflows optimized for speed and clarity.

    Key Scenarios We Designed For:

    Budget Allocation

    GMs need to allocate quarterly budgets across departments, view spending trends, and adjust allocations based on seasonality and historical data.

    Expense Approvals

    Approval workflows need to be visible, trackable, and fast with mobile access for managers on the go.

    Monthly & Yearly Analysis

    Regional and VP-level users need comparative dashboards with filtering by property, time period, and expense category.




    Wireframes

    We started with low-fidelity wireframes to quickly test structural concepts with stakeholders. These early iterations focused on dashboard hierarchy, navigation patterns, and data density critical factors for a financial platform where clarity is paramount.

    Feedback from general managers revealed that they needed faster access to approval queues and budget summaries. Regional managers wanted more prominent comparison tools. These insights shaped multiple rounds of iteration before moving to high-fidelity design.

    High-Fidelity Design

    The final designs prioritize data clarity and workflow efficiency. We developed role-specific dashboards that surface the most relevant information first, with progressive disclosure for deeper analysis. Charts and filters were designed for immediate comprehension, avoiding visual clutter that could obscure financial insights.

    Collaboration with engineers ensured that our design specs translated accurately to production. We established a component library with detailed interaction states, responsive behaviors, and accessibility standards.

    Reflection

    This project reinforced the importance of designing for multiple user roles with fundamentally different needs. Balancing the complexity of enterprise financial data with the need for intuitive, fast workflows required constant iteration and stakeholder validation. The biggest challenge was resisting the temptation to add features that sounded useful but didn't directly support core workflows. Staying focused on the scenarios that mattered most—budget allocation, approvals, and analysis—resulted in a platform that felt purposeful rather than overwhelming.
